  1. Paragraph for Graph 1

When we compare similarities in twins, twins share the same DNA, so we would expect their results to show similarities. Different companies would produce differing results so I resulted to compare the twins based on each company used. In this way we are given results to compare pending on which company you want to use, 23 and me or ancestry. What was intended was to compare the bars for Twin A and Twin B within each region which have similarities regardless of which DNA kit is selected, but in our results we see that the twins themselves are not the source of variation in these results.

Paragraph for Graph 2

This time we attempt to explicitly compare the kits across companies and with each other. And as said before while the twins may agree with each other, the three DNA testing companies do not always agree with themselves. Looking across each twin’s facet, the bars for 23andMe, Ancestry, and MyHeritage frequently tell different stories for the same person. Ancestry tends to report more extreme values, pushing dominant regions higher and minor regions lower, while 23andMe and MyHeritage often report a more distributed breakdown.

Challenge#3: Net promoter Score

Comparison to other businesses

Over time the business Net Promoter Score shows a gradual improvement compared to its competitors. Eventhough some competitors still remain relatively stable our score trends upward, indicating stronger growth in customer satisfaction. In February 2020, the business NPS is among the higher scores compared to others this directly suggest we are performing competitively within the market.

Changes in NPS components

The increase in our Net Promoter Score over time is driven by a rise in the percentage of promoters and a decrease in detractors. This surely indicates that more customers are becoming highly satisfied with our business and fewer customers are dissatisfied. The percentage of passive customers remains relatively stable which directly suggest that most of the improvement comes from converting detractors into promoters.

My choice of graphic was a map as a primary view because the data was very much geographic, with airport position encoded spatially. I used the size of each mark to encode the route count on the airport circles. I used color and opacity to encode selection state so when you click an airport it turn red and fully opaque while others recede. Route lines appear only on click rather than all at once. I minimized used of multi colors to avoid it being termed as ugly. I made sure my graph told a story as first glance as people would be able to recognize the red dots on the big graph.

I got this my data set airports and flight-airport from the github page that had vega lite data sets. https://github.com/vega/vega-datasets/tree/main/data .
